Robotic Gripping Arm

I designed and developed a 4-DOF robotic arm for light material handling, aimed at assisting production lines in industrial environments. The arm was capable of performing accurate lifting tasks and was controlled via a laptop or PC.

The components were fabricated using 3D printing, which allowed for precise dimensions and significantly reduced both manufacturing time and cost. The arm's movement was driven by 4 stepper motors, providing smooth articulation across joints.

The control system was built using Arduino hardware and software, enabling programmable and flexible operation of the arm. After testing and validation, the robotic arm successfully demonstrated reliable performance for light-duty lifting tasks.

Key Features:

  • 4 Degrees of Freedom (DOF) for flexible arm movement
  • 3D-printed structure for precision and cost-efficiency
  • Stepper motors for smooth and accurate joint control
  • Arduino-based control system for easy programming and customization
  • Remote control via laptop or PC

Skills & Tools: 3D Printing, Mechanical Design, Stepper Motors, Embedded Systems
